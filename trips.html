Our Trips

Two trip types. One standard of progression.

School trips built for curriculum, compliance, and confidence — and progression trips built for individuals who want to genuinely improve on snow. Both delivered to the same exacting standard.

🏫 School Ski Trips 🎿 Progression Trips

First Track offers two distinct programmes. Use the links above to jump straight to the one that fits you, or scroll through to understand both — many families choose to combine pre-trip indoor preparation with an Alpine progression week regardless of which programme they select.

Schools & Groups

School Ski Trips

Fully managed, week-long programmes combining lodge accommodation, structured ski instruction, lift passes, transfers, and evening activities — tailored to your school's curriculum and budget. Delivered compliantly and professionally, every time.

What's included

🏠

Lodge accommodation

En-suite rooms in handpicked alpine lodges, with full board and a structured evening programme. Lovell Camp & Academy in Gstaad is our flagship school partner.

⛷️

Structured ski instruction

Up to 5 hours of instruction per day with English-speaking, qualified instructors. Groups are ability-banded and given daily objectives — not just pointed down the mountain.

🎫

Lift passes

Full-area lift passes for the duration of the trip, included in the all-in package price.

🚌

Transfers & transport

Airport-to-lodge transfers arranged and managed. We work with partners who have their own minibus fleets and established transfer networks.

🌙

Evening activities

A structured après-ski programme — swimming, ice-skating, climbing, and cultural activities — keeping pupils engaged and well-supervised after the slopes close.

📋

Parent communications

Templates, parent letters, and information packs to make the school's internal process as smooth as possible.

Risk assessments, insurance certificates, and safeguarding documentation available for school review on request.

Teens & Adults

Progression Trips

Small-group, intensive on-snow improvement programmes for motivated individuals who want to genuinely advance their skiing. Whether you're a teen looking to break through a plateau, an adult who trained at Skieasy and is ready for real snow, or a complete beginner who wants to learn properly — these trips are designed for you.

For motivated young skiers

Youth & Teen Progression Weeks

Small-group, intensive on-snow improvement programmes for motivated teenage skiers. Built around daily skill assessment and a structured end-of-week progression review — so participants know exactly where they started, and how far they've come.

Small groups of motivated, similar-ability teens
Daily skill objectives set and reviewed each evening
End-of-week formal progression assessment
Qualified English-speaking instruction throughout
Structured lodge accommodation and supervision
For adult learners & improvers

Adult Progression Trips

Curated group weeks for adult beginners and improvers. Particularly well-suited to clients of Skieasy (Chiswick) who have built indoor technique on the treadmill and are ready to translate that into real Alpine skiing.

Ideal for Skieasy graduates entering real snow
Curated small-group format for focused improvement
Structured week with daily coaching and review
Social, supportive group atmosphere for adults
Exclusive partner pathway for Skieasy clients
Recommended add-on for all progression participants

Pre-Trip Indoor Preparation — Skieasy, London

We strongly recommend that all progression trip participants — particularly beginners and early improvers — complete a block of Skieasy sessions before travelling to the Alps. Skieasy's revolving ski treadmill in Chiswick, London, allows participants to build muscle memory, learn to stop and turn, and arrive on snow with genuine foundations.

Year-round, weatherproof indoor lessons
1:1 instruction on a revolving ski treadmill
Claims learning "up to 8x faster" than slopes
Mirrored walls for technique self-analysis
Min age 4 (ski), 7 (snowboard)
Bundled into First Track packages at a group rate

What progression trips include

🏔️

Lodge or hotel accommodation

Comfortable, well-located accommodation — chosen for proximity to terrain suited to intensive progression work.

⛷️

Expert instruction — every day

Qualified, English-speaking instructors who understand progression goals, not just lesson delivery. Groups are ability-matched and size is kept small.

📊

Daily assessment & feedback

Each day ends with a skill review — what worked, what to focus on tomorrow, and how the group is progressing collectively.

🎫

Lift passes & transfers

Full-area passes and airport transfers included in the package price.

📝

End-of-week progression review

A formal written progression review for each participant — what level they started at, what they achieved, and recommended next steps.
